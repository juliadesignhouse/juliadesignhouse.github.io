JULIA YANG

About Me

I am currently pursuing a Master of Information at the University of Toronto, concentrating on User Experience Design. During my undergraduate years, I studied a double major in Communication, Culture, Information & Technology (CCIT) and Technology, Coding & Society (TCS) at the University of Toronto Mississauga.

Growing up in a loving family, the first lesson I learned was to have a passion for helping others. My family ran some restaurants, serving various people near that area. Whenever someone couldn’t afford a meal, they would receive a free or discounted meal after sharing their story. This past experience gave me a deep understanding of the complexities of human experience, as I believe everyone has a unique story. This has always influenced my life, and a strong sense of empathy to help others has always guided my path.

As a freshman undergraduate, I was at first uncertain about my future career. After rethinking my life, I soon realized that my passion for art and my desire to help and improve people's lives perfectly matched becoming a UX designer. After taking Human-Computer Interaction and Communication, I’m even more convinced this is my field because good design can prevent people from dying in car accidents. I have now made my way further by pursuing a master’s degree focusing on UXD, but this is not the end; I’m just starting my journey to help people and solve their problems with my trained skills.

More About My Artistic Side

I’m an art person in many ways. I love browsing art galleries, understanding the abstract meaning and the emotions from different works of art. This is also where my creativity always comes from. I’m also passionate about visiting local museums while traveling. To me, this is a great way to learn about cultures worldwide in depth.

I’m also a piano player! I’m deeply in love with many different types of music, which makes my everyday life full of happiness and joy!
